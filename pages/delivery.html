Mission Critical for Microsoft Security Cloud Capabilities

Three pillars that define the MCSfMSC service delivery experience for our customers

Technical
Engagement

Assigned Microsoft Security Engineering Customer Lead who advocates within Security Engineering and across Microsoft to represent the customer interests
Understanding of your security products and environment with extensive knowledge of the Microsoft Security portfolio

Reliability

Security engineering enhanced awareness and readiness during changes, critical projects, and major events to remove technical blockers, enabling quicker issue mitigation
Insights from the Security engineering roadmap to proactively prepare for upcoming changes and leverage those insights for strategic planning

Accelerated
Resolution

15-min Sev 1/A, and expert response by an advanced Microsoft Security support engineer on all support issue severities
Engineering-led crisis management with experts that provide up-to-date communication on the incident mitigation
Ability to self-escalate directly to Security Engineering for Sev 1/A issues to receive the highest level of prioritization from the Microsoft Security engineering and feature teams
Comprehensive Root Cause Analysis of the customer critical issue when requested within 10 business days of the request and create targeted improvements to prevent future issues and enhance your security product use

Delivery KPIs & Metrics

Standard metrics tracked for MCSfMSC service delivery engagements

Initial Response Time (IRT)

1st call response time from case creation, per MCSfMSC severity classification.

Sev 1: 15 min
Sev A: 15 min
Sev B: 2 hours
Sev C: 4 hours

Secure Score Improvement

Customer's Microsoft Secure Score improvement over the engagement period.

Target: +15% in 90 days

Root Cause Analysis (RCA)

Comprehensive RCA delivered when customer explicitly requests within the defined window.

SLA: 10 business days

Customer Satisfaction (CSAT)

Customer satisfaction rating collected after each milestone and at QBR.

Target: ≥ 4.5 / 5.0

Operational Guidelines

Best practices and guidelines for MCSfMSC service delivery engineers

Communication Standards
  • Weekly Status Emails: Send every Friday by 3 PM with accomplishments, plans, risks
  • Incident Communication: Notify customer within SLA response time, provide updates per severity cadence
  • Escalation Notification: Immediately inform SDM when escalating to L3/L4
  • Documentation: All customer interactions must be logged in the engagement tracker
  • Teams Channel: Maintain a dedicated Teams channel per customer engagement
Security & Compliance
  • Data Handling: Never store customer data on personal devices or unauthorized locations
  • Access Management: Use just-in-time access; revoke access when engagement ends
  • NDA Compliance: All customer information is confidential per engagement NDA
  • Change Management: All production changes require customer approval and CAB review
  • Audit Trail: Document all configuration changes with before/after states
Quality Standards
  • Peer Review: All deliverables must be peer-reviewed before customer delivery
  • Template Compliance: Use approved templates for all customer-facing documents
  • Testing: Validate all configurations in test environment before production deployment
  • Rollback Plan: Document rollback procedures for every change implementation
  • Knowledge Capture: Document learnings and add to knowledge base after resolution
