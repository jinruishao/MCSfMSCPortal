Severity Classification

Classify issues by severity to determine the correct escalation path and SLA

Sev 1

Catastrophic Business Impact

Complete loss of the mission critical business process. Service halted or cannot continue. Needs immediate attention.

Response Time: 1st call response in 15 minutes
Engineering: Customer Lead Engineer Engaged
Effort: 24x7 continuous effort
Escalation: Engineering led crisis management

Customer Responsibility:

  • Use Direct Connect Escalation
  • Notification of senior executives
  • Allocation of appropriate resources
  • 24x7 continuous effort
  • Rapid access & response from change control authority
Sev A

Critical Business Impact

Significant loss or degradation of mission critical business process. Service cannot reasonably continue. Needs urgent attention.

Response Time: 1st call response in 15 minutes
Engineering: Customer Lead Engineer Engaged
Effort: 24x7 continuous effort
Escalation: Engineering led crisis management

Customer Responsibility:

  • Use Direct Connect Escalation
  • Management notification
  • Allocation of appropriate resources
  • 24x7 continuous effort
  • Rapid access & response from change control authority
Sev B

Moderate Business Impact

Moderate loss or degradation of mission critical business process. Service can reasonably continue in an impaired manner.

Response Time: 1st call response within 2 hours
Resources: Allocation of appropriate resources
Escalation: Rapid access & response from change control authority

Customer Responsibility:

  • Allocation of appropriate resources
  • Rapid access & response from change control authority
Sev C

Minimum Business Impact

Minor or no loss of mission critical business process. Service can continue with minor or no impediments.

Response Time: 1st call response within 4 hours
Effort: Effort during business hours only
Resources: Allocation of appropriate resources
Resolution: Response within 24 hours

Customer Responsibility:

  • Allocation of appropriate resources
  • Response within 24 hours
